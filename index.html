Health Inequity Statistics

According to the National Institutes of Health (NIH), health inequities are a significant concern:

  • Racial and Ethnic Disparities: People of color often face higher rates of chronic conditions such as diabetes and heart disease compared to white populations.
  • Access to Healthcare: Disparities in access to healthcare can lead to delayed treatments and poorer health outcomes.
  • Income and Education: Individuals with lower income and education levels are at greater risk for health problems and less likely to receive preventive care.
